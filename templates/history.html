The Legend of Kashi

Varanasi, or Kashi, is widely regarded as the most sacred city in Hinduism. It is situated on the crescent-shaped left bank of the holy Ganges. Mark Twain famously described it as "older than history."

The Origins

According to Hindu mythology, the city was founded by Lord Shiva himself. During a fight between Brahma and Shiva, one of Brahma's five heads was torn off by Shiva. As a result, the head stuck to Shiva's hand. He then traveled to Kashi, where the head fell off, and his sin was washed away in the Ganges.

Key Eras of Varanasi:

  • The Vedic Period: A center of Aryan religion and philosophy.
  • 6th Century BCE: Buddha founded Buddhism nearby in Sarnath.
  • The Mughal Era: A time of both architectural destruction and cultural resilience.
  • Modern Era: A global center for Yoga, Music, and Spirituality.

A City of Learning

For centuries, Varanasi has been a hub of Sanskrit scholarship. Students from all over the world come here to study the Vedas, classical music, and traditional Indian medicine (Ayurveda).
